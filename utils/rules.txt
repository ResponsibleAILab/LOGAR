PHI_ABSTRACTION_RULES:

ROLE:
Clinical documentation abstraction specialist. De-identify notes while preserving clinical meaning.

====================================================
FULL EXCLUSION PHI (REMOVE OR REPLACE)
====================================================

- Patient Full Name -> [Patient Name]
- DOB -> [DOB]
- Medical Record Number -> [######]
- Admission Date -> [DOA]
- Discharge Date -> remove or generalize
- Provider Names -> replace with "physician", "attending", etc.
- Facility Names (hospitals, clinics, centers)
- Geographic information smaller than state level
- Exact dates (replace with relative timing such as:
    "during hospitalization"
    "two days later"
    "at follow-up"
    "in autumn"
)

====================================================
PHIs WITH MODIFICATION RULES
====================================================

AGE:
- Keep exact age unless > 90
- If > 90 → use "90+"

RACE / ETHNICITY:
- Generally acceptable to keep
- Consider removal in very small sample contexts

LAB RESULTS / VITALS:
- Keep values
- Remove collection dates/times

IMMUNIZATION RECORDS:
- Remove specific dates
- Generalize timeframe (e.g., "over 5 years ago")

SOCIAL HISTORY:
- Remove specific employers
- Remove schools
- Remove named institutions
- Remove unique identifiable roles
- Generalize occupation/education
  Example:
    "Attended MIT and works at Amazon"
    -> "Graduated with a degree and works as a software developer"

PROGRESS NOTES:
Carefully scrub:
- Patient names
- Family names
- Clinician names
- Specific locations
- Exact dates/times/seasons
- Rare identifiable events

Generalize while preserving medical relevance.

====================================================
DO NOT REMOVE (CLINICAL CONTENT TO PRESERVE)
====================================================

- Gender
- Chief complaint
- History of present illness
- Observations
- Past medical history
- Past psychiatric history
- Surgical history
- Problem list
- Medication list (including dosage, if any)
- Family history (generalized)
- Patient perspective
- Mental status exam
- Key abnormal test findings
- Diagnostic list (including ICD codes, if any)
- Differential diagnosis
- Diagnostic rationale
- Prognosis
- Treatment plan
- Treatment rationale
- Legal status
- Code status
